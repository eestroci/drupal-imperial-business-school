Our Full-Time MBA

Grow as a leader

Developing your own Personal Leadership Journey is an essential part of your MBA. The LEADS module will help you gain the tools to become a responsible leader of inclusive and sustainable organisations.

Study in London

Study in a global hub for business and finance, learn across our South Kensington and White City campuses, and benefit from our relationships with major employers, offering valuable industry connections and helping you build a global perspective.

 

Enhance your global business experience

Take advantage of global experiences to explore new business environments while you study. On the Imperial MBA, you will gain cultural awareness and an understanding of global markets. This will help develop a global business mindset.

Personalise your MBA

Choose from flexcore modules, electives, and concentrations to shape the Imperial MBA for your career ambitions. Develop your knowledge and understanding across a range of topics, from ESG or entrepreneurship to digital strategy and transformation.

Connect with industry leaders

As an Imperial MBA student, you will engage in consulting assignments, innovation projects, and internships working with real organisations. You will also connect with industry mentors and advisors through our industry-sponsored research centres.

Programme content

Personalise the Imperial MBA according to your career ambitions. Our one-year MBA includes pre-study and core modules that ground your knowledge in fundamental topics. In addition, a  choice of flexcore and elective modules then allow you personalise your programme.

You can also choose a concentration to focus on a particular subject area and signal your interests to employers.

We are pleased to offer an optional extended term, that will allow you to extend your programme duration to 16 months. This gives you the opportunity to commit to a longer work placement or global exchange. In turn, you’ll gain deeper work experience, global experience and earn additional credits. Please note, the extended term is subject to an additional tuition fee.

Please note, programme content is subject to change.

View an example Full-Time MBA timetable

Through a combination of online pre-study modules and induction week, you will be ready to hit the ground running when your programme begins. You’ll be prepared for the academic rigour ahead, ensuring you're equipped to maximise the benefits of your programme.

Pre-study modules

Online | Pre-September

You will be expected to complete online pre-study modules before the start of your programme. These will be delivered via Canvas, our virtual learning environment (VLE). These include primers in key areas including accounting, careers, corporate sustainability, data analysis, finance, maths, plagiarism awareness, and social innovation and ethics.

Please note that some modules are compulsory while others are strongly recommended.

Induction week

On campus | Early September, compulsory

Your Full-Time MBA induction will be spent at our South Kensington campus in London. Here, you will meet with the rest of your cohort, faculty, and programme staff. This takes place at the start of September when the programme begins and lasts for two weeks. It will include a climate change away day - an opportunity to bond with your new classmates.

Reflecting modern career requirements, our core modules provide you with the basis of what you need to grow as a leader. This core learning strikes a balance between analytical, knowledge-oriented disciplines and subjects that emphasise vision, skills, and judgement.

Business Analytics

September-January

Managers are faced with a wealth of data. This module is about turning that data into actionable insights, leading to well-informed decisions for complex business problems. Learn how to build a business situation that recognises its essential, and takes account of uncertainty.

Business Problem Solving

September-January

This module equips you with a range of frameworks, tools, and techniques. Many of these have been developed by the management consulting industry. The focus of the module will be problems that include the collection and analysis of information/data.

Corporate Finance 1

September-January

Corporate finance matters, both to the success of organisations, and to economies. The business world is affected and, at times, influenced by the financial decisions made by corporations. It is important to understand the major types of financial decisions made by corporations, and how these decisions affect your work.

You will develop a market-oriented framework for analysing a corporation’s investment decisions. You’ll also understand how these decisions impact your business.

Financial and Management Accounting

September-January

This module will take you beyond the numbers and teach you how a company’s financial reporting can help you understand a company’s health and story.

Innovation and Entrepreneurship

September-January

This module provides you with a unique opportunity to build your knowledge, skills, and experience in starting up an innovative business, looking for critical funding, and structuring a deal to make your business viable.

You will learn how to identify approaches to overcoming innovation problems, understand customer need and value proposal, evaluate commercial potential, tactics for securing buy-in, and best practices for scaling your business.

Managerial Economics

September-January

Whatever industry you’re in, in order to ensure your business stays buoyant in a competitive market, you need to understand how individual markets operate, and how to use economic tools to analyse your position.

By the end of this module, you will know how to apply basic principles of economics to real-world questions, such as how markets do or do not work and the choice of pricing by firms.

Marketing

September-January

Develop a general management viewpoint in planning and evaluating marketing decisions, from strategic and tactical perspectives. It will help you understand how marketing decisions contribute to business performance and how such decisions are affected by organisational and environmental influences.

Organisational Behaviour

September-January

Gain a practical, theoretically-grounded collection of tools you can use to be an effective leader and manager.

You will understand how to:

  • Manage yourself and your own career
  • Lead and motivate others
  • Work effectively in teams to deliver good results

Strategy

September-January

You will explore business-level and corporate strategy, and develop your ability to think beyond day-to-day business activities.

You will examine issues such as:

  • Defining a firm’s purpose
  • Understanding its competitors
  • How to gain and maintain an edge
     
Personalise your MBA and deep dive into a specialist area with flexcore modules. You will take two or more flexcore modules, depending on your choice of electives.

Data Analytics and Visualisation for Business

February-March

This module covers a number of tools on an advanced level for data manipulation and visualisation. This will include Excel, Google Sheets/Query, Power BI and Tableau. There will  be an emphasis on how to ask insightful business questions and analyse data interactively.

While studying these tools, you will take a managerial perspective and develop a statistical thinking when analysing the data. Discussion will be complemented by real data sets from IT, supply chain, retail, finance, and other relevant industries.

Design Thinking: Solving Complex Problems Through a Disruptive Mindset

February-March

Design Thinking represents a powerful way to unleash the creative potential of managers and leaders in organisations. It is rapidly becoming a new management paradigm for value creation. You will develop the ability to:

  • Gain deep insights about users
  • Define and reframe problems
  • Effectively generate solutions or alternative approaches

You will learn how to clearly articulate ideas and concepts verbally and visually. You’ll also develop a mindset that embraces uncertainty and seeks new opportunities by exploration and experimentation.

Corporate Finance II

February-March

Some say risk is an inevitable part of investing. Risky investments can bring big rewards, but these risks can, and should, be managed. To prevent loss, you must understand the main financial securities and markets, and how to develop efficient investment strategies.

To complement your classroom studies, you’ll have access to the Connect learning platform’s state-of-the-art technology. This includes a fully interactive book with online exercises and assignments.

Macroeconomics

February-March

This module analyses the macroeconomic backdrop against which businesses and governments operate. You’ll analyse how the decisions of firms and consumers generate the aggregate outcomes that macroeconomists focus on. For example, total output (GDP), employment, inflation and the balance of payments.

The module will examine the 2008 financial crisis. You will explore how it has affected economies and prompted responses from governments and central banks. Looking to a current and very local issue, you will also explore the economics of Brexit, right in the city where the decisions are being made.

Operations

February-March

A firm can create competitive advantage through efficient and effective management of its operations. This module covers key concepts of operations management, including:

  • The analysis of process flows and bottlenecks
  • Capacity planning
  • Inventory and supply chain management
  • Quality management

You will understand how to apply these ideas to achieve operational excellence and total quality.

With optional concentrations, you can develop your expertise in a primary area, and craft the MBA to align with your goals. Depending on your company or career path, a concentration can give you an edge in a competitive job market.

Analytics & Operations

Lead: Dr. Gah-Yi Ban

Organisations are collecting more and more data. Therefore, it’s increasingly important for executives to be literate in state-of-the-art techniques for data collection, analysis and visualisation. Discover the latest thinking and techniques in the field of business analytics from coding to data analysis.

Strategy and Digital Transformation

Lead: Prof. Chris Tucci

This concentration is designed for those seeking roles in technology or the digital transformation of products, services, and processes. Elective options range from AI ventures and digital business model design and digital transformation.

Innovation & Entrepreneurship

Lead: Prof. Markus Perkmann

This concentration is designed for those who want to either:

  • Innovate and launch products from within existing corporations
  • Found their own ventures
  • Join a high-growth firm

Elective options may include design thinking, entrepreneurial finance, and completing a start-up project as part of the Entrepreneurial Journey module.

Environmental, social and governance (ESG) and stakeholder impact

Lead: Prof Eduardo Andrade

Dive deep into the mindset, knowledge, and skills required to lead and transform organisations towards greater sustainability and stakeholder impact. You may take this concentration if you are aiming for one of the growing number of specialist roles that corporations, consultancies, NGOs and governments offer in this area.

Electives include sustainable strategy, ESG-related finance, sustainable and social innovation, sustainable consumption, and energy business.

Finance

Lead: Prof. Andrea Buraschi

If you are looking to work in financial services, corporate finance or fintech, take the finance concentration. Electives include asset management, private equity, entrepreneurial finance, sustainable/ESG finance and fintech, among others.

As a UK MBA student, you can get a true taste for life in London, one of the most desirable cities in the world! You’ll also enjoy opportunities to travel internationally and immerse yourself in new business environments. Learn about yourself, new cultures and gain an understanding of how businesses operate around the world.

Global Experience Week

Summer, compulsory

Global Experience Week is an unforgettable opportunity that MBA students look forward to each year. You’ll learn about the challenges local organisations face, and how their operations are impacted by their country’s business environment.  

Past Global Experience Week destinations have included the Nordics, Brazil and Kenya. Students explored how businesses can be transformed to tackle climate action.

Locations are subject to change. Specific dates and destinations for your cohort will be announced during the Autumn term.

As this is a compulsory element of your programme, the cost of this trip is included in your tuition fees.

IB Glocal Electives

Summer, optional

The IB Glocal Electives teach students how to explore contemporary global business problems with a local perspective. You will gain a deeper insight into international issues in a particular geography. You’ll also benefit from the in-market exposure and teaching the trips provide.  

These are led by the Imperial Business School faculty in collaboration with partners at global locations. Offered as intensive four-day study abroad programmes, they enrich the academic curriculum by providing a stimulating experience. Previous locations have included Houston, Milan, Singapore and Tel Aviv.

Global Exchanges

Optional

The optional Global Exchanges programme programme allows you to increase your international exposure and build your network. You’ll be able to take advantage of specialised electives at one of our partner schools.

If you opt for the extended term, you will have the opportunity to undertake a term-long exchange at one of our partner schools. This would run in the Autumn term in addition to the other elements of your programme. Please note that additional fees apply.

Throughout your time on the Imperial MBA, we’ll support your development as a leader. You’ll learn how your awareness of ethical, societal, and diversity issues, and your own leadership style, play a role in every decision you make. Leadership is an essential part of the Imperial MBA, with several components running throughout the year.

Personal Leadership Journey

Year-long, compulsory

The Personal Leadership Journey is a Careers-led module that runs throughout the programme. It is designed to give you the key skills you need as a global professional through workshops, reflective exercises and one-to-one meetings.

It will also enable you to make new connections with leaders and experts from the industries that most interest you.

Working closely with your own personal careers consultant throughout the year, you’ll focus on four key areas including:

  • Personal impact and networking
  • Executive communication
  • Managing your career
  • Leading in a complex and connected world 

LEADS (Leadership, Ethics, Awareness, Diversity, Society)

Year-long, compulsory

The world needs responsible leaders who care about the long-term health of their organisations, society, and the environment. Our holistic LEADS module runs all year, and starts by giving you the tools to discover and analyse yourself.

You will then consider yourself in relation to others, and explore the role of leaders in making organisations sustainable.

The Imperial MBA offers many opportunities to apply what you’ve learnt in the classroom to real-life scenarios. Gain greater confidence in your abilities and put your leadership skills to the test.

Imperial Innovation Challenge

October

In this one-week learning laboratory, you’ll work with Imperial scientists. Together, you’ll apply innovative thinking to generate business solutions to complex international issues. In groups, you will use business model innovation to create economic disruption with positive environmental and social effects. Students typically work with a deep tech client, developing their understanding of the relationship between business and technology.

Careers Week

Careers Week enables you to focus on your career goals through a series of workshops and activities. You’ll also receive tailored support and feedback to action.

Highlights of the week include:

  • Tailored career sessions
  • Drop-ins for sector-specific career guidance
  • Entrepreneurs Day, co-delivered by the Imperial Enterprise Lab and Imperial Hackspace
  • Interview practice and feedback
  • External one-to-ones for specific sectors

Global Insights Speaker Series

You have the option to attend a speaker series with industry leaders, as well as intimate seminars with Imperial experts from fields including climate change, AI and fintech.

Full-Time MBA Capstone

Your MBA will culminate in an unforgettable capstone experience away from campus. You’ll spend time in a natural environment, giving you the space to reflect upon your learning experience. In previous years, students visited a national park in Wales with their classmates while the class of 2022/23 visited Norway.

Over the spring and summer terms, you will select one group project. You will choose between the Entrepreneurial Journey, Strategic Consulting Project, Analytics Project or Finance Project. In July and August, you will also complete an individual project. You will have the choice of a Business Project or internship.

Entrepreneurial Journey

Min. eight weeks

The Entrepreneurial Journey is your opportunity to work an idea into a product or service. You’ll pitch your proposition to a panel of venture capitalists, and compete for a monetary prize! You can connect with Imperial scientists if your idea is tech-related.

The journey is suitable for intrapreneurs, or those wanting to start their own ventures. You will:

  • Develop a value proposition
  • Carry out customer discovery research
  • Validate the product or service
  • Create a fully-fledged business proposal

Call on the Imperial Enterprise Lab to help you connect with coaches and investors.

Strategic Consulting Project

March-June

The Strategic Consulting Project (SCP) module provides the opportunity to work on a real-world business problem for a real client, in a team setting.

This will allow you to apply the knowledge you gained in the Business Problem Solving module, your other core modules (depending on the topic you are addressing), and your skill in working as part of a team, which is supported by a faculty coach. Clients tend to be a mix of large and small companies. Recent clients include Virgin Media, SSE, Rajasthan Royals, and Arrival.

Analytics Project

Approx. 10 weeks

Gain practical experience in developing business/data analytics applications for business with our MBA Analytics Project. Work part-time in small groups to complete a business-related project which contains a significant analytics component.

Financial Consulting Project

Min. eight weeks

Choose between working individually or in a team of two to address an important and real-life problem, opportunity, or challenge in a finance firm. Your selected project will be amenable to an approach based on data gathering and analysis. Teams will prepare a formal written proposal and communicate their results via both presentation and written report.

Business Project

The Business Project allows you to develop your ideas on a particular topic of interest. You may choose from the following three options: 

  1. Complete a research project with an organisation of interest.
  2. Complete a consulting report covering a sector or market and one or more organisations.
  3. Develop your own business plan to gain more entrepreneurial experience.

Internship

Employ your networking skills to organise an internship. Our Careers team will alert you to opportunities arising from our corporate connections and alumni network.

Extended Work Placement

2-6 months, Optional

If you opt for the extended term, you will have the opportunity to undertake an extended work placement. Please note that additional fees apply.

Frequently asked questions

To be eligible to apply for the programme, you would normally require: 

  • Three years’ relevant postgraduate work experience. In exceptional circumstances, we will consider candidates with less than three years’ experience who can demonstrate strong academic performance, excellent career progression and international work experience.
  • You will need at least a UK Second Class Honours undergraduate degree or international equivalent or an acceptable professional qualification
  • English language test results (if applicable). 

Read more information about entry requirements

We review our requirements annually. Updates usually happen in September each year before the next academic year of entry.

All applications to our postgraduate programmes are made online – we do not accept paper applications.

Please refer to the how to apply section of our Admissions page to see which documents you are required to submit with your application.

We have a series of admissions rounds throughout the year. Decisions are typically released four weeks after each application deadline. Full details of application and decision deadlines are available here.

Please email the Imperial MBA Recruitment & Admissions team if you would like to request feedback on your application. Please note that whilst limited feedback can be provided, the decision of the academic selectors is final. It will not be possible for your application to be reconsidered.

You are required to have a valid GMAT or GRE score as part of your application for the Full-Time MBA.

For the GMAT Exam you will need a minimum overall score of 555. If you have taken the GMAT Exam (10th Edition), you will need a score of at least 600 (our current average is 666). Scores are valid for five years from your appointment date.

If you are taking the GRE, we recommend a minimum score of 156 in the verbal, and 158 in the quantitative sections.

You are required to have a valid GMAT or GRE score as part of your application for the Imperial MBA.

If you have applied for and been granted a waiver, then you may submit your application without a GMAT or GRE score. If you do not hold a waiver, you are required to submit a valid GMAT or GRE score as part of your application.

Your application will not be complete until this is received, and therefore will not be processed by our Admissions Team and may be withdrawn.

To submit a GMAT score, you must request for it to be shared with Imperial Business School online using the code S22-7S-16. Please note that you do not need to send a hard copy unless requested.

To submit a GRE score, you must request for it to be shared with Imperial Business School online using the code 0121.

GMAT Exam scores will remain valid for five years from your appointment date.

For each module, you will be expected to complete approximately 12-20 hours of class time. Additionally, you will need to spend three hours for every one hour in the classroom.

Our programmes are intensive periods of study. Contact hours form only part of the activity that you will be asked to undertake. You will be required to devote time to: 

  • Preparing for lectures
  • Undertaking background reading
  • Working in groups
  • Completing assessments (both individual and in groups)
  • Revising for exams

A significant amount of time will also be allocated to career planning and development . You’ll also be expected to actively contribute to the Business School community.

This is not an exhaustive list. However, it provides you with a flavour of what you can expect from your time as a student at Imperial Business School.

For information on the fee and deposit amount, please visit the Admissions page.

You can pay your tuition fees in one instalment at the start of term. If you are self-funding, you can pay in two instalments, at the start of term and in February.

If you pay in two instalments, a 2% surcharge of the total amount will be added to your first instalment. You will be asked to select a payment schedule during the offer acceptance process.

Please contact the Tuition Fee team if you have any further questions.

If an organisation is sponsoring you and will be responsible for paying your tuition fees, you must provide acceptable proof of the award.

Satisfactory evidence of sponsorship is usually regarded as an original, dated and duly authorised letter from your sponsor on headed paper which includes the following:

  • Your name and College Identifier Number
  • The programme for which you have been offered a place
  • The amount of fees which will be paid by your sponsor (please report in GBP)
  • The address to which the invoice should be sent
  • Any Purchase Order Number required
  • Confirmation that the fees will be sent directly to Imperial College

Sponsorship letters should be emailed to both the MBA Admissions team and Tuition Fees team to the following email addresses: mba@imperial.ac.uk and tuition.fees@imperial.ac.uk

We will start to issue CAS from April. The details of your CAS will appear on your My Imperial record after you’ve met all the conditions of your offer. Please note that you will need to upload a scanned copy of the photo page of your passport and current visa (if you have recently completed a degree in the UK), before your CAS can be issued. Please upload these documents to the ‘passport/visa’ section of your My Imperial account.

Once your CAS is ready, you will receive an email confirming that it has been uploaded onto your My Imperial account. If you log into My Imperial you will be able to find your CAS number and the details that have been included in your CAS.

We cannot provide you with a CAS if you have outstanding conditions on your offer. You must allow enough time to meet your conditions and apply for a visa before the start date of your programme of study.

Your CAS number holds information about the programme you will study and if you have paid any fees. You will not need a formal letter from the University confirming your offer status for your visa appointment.

For more information about what you will need, see the UKVI website.

If you need any advice about your visa please contact the International Student Support team. Most information can be found on the International Student Support website.
